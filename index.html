About the Project

The BIONIC project seeks to revolutionize security and critical infrastructure protection through the development of a biohybrid intelligent system that mimics the behavior of a real brain.

Leveraging neuromorphic and biomimetic computing architectures, the project will emulate the brain's in vivo functionality, creating an adaptive computing platform capable of learning and being trained by a real biological brain.

By integrating biologically realistic spiking neural network models with real-time neuromorphic hardware, BIONIC aims to reproduce key features of brain dynamics such as adaptability, robustness, and recovery after damage.

We translate real brain dynamics into biologically grounded spiking neural network models. By tuning neurons, synapses, connectivity, and plasticity, we reproduce healthy and damaged brain behavior in both software and real-time hardware.

  • Data-driven SNN models of brain networks

Adaptive Biohybrid Neuromorphic Systems

Closing the loop between living and artificial networks
‚åÑ

We integrate biological and artificial neural systems into a unified real-time platform. Through closed-loop interaction and damage-response experiments, we demonstrate adaptability, resilience, and self-repair.

  • Real-time encoding of biological signals into spikes
  • Bidirectional interaction between brain tissue and SNNs
  • Validation of adaptability and resilience under damage

Publications & Dissemination

The BIONIC project is committed to open science and broad dissemination of its results. Scientific findings, datasets, and software tools developed throughout the project will be shared with the research community, end-users, and the general public.

  • Peer-reviewed journal publications and conference papers
  • Open-access software and datasets
  • Scientific workshops, conferences, and public events
  • Engagement with end-users and technology transfer offices
